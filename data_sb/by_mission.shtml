SBN Data by "Mission"

The Small Bodies Node supports and archives data from a number of spacecraft missions as well as other programs, investigations, and surveys. A list of missions along with the type of small body data that SBN has is provided below.

Planetary and Small Body Missions

These are missions whose primary purpose was to explore planets and/or small bodies.

  • BOPPS: C/2013 A1 (Siding Spring), C/2012 K1 (PanSTARRS), C/2014 E2 (Jacques), (1) Ceres
  • BRRISON: C/2012 S1 (ISON)
  • Cassini: Interplanetary dust, small satellites of Saturn
  • Contour: Safed ground calibrations
  • Double Asteroid Redirection Test (DART): Near-Earth binary asteroid Didymos, asteroid deflection test
  • Dawn: 1 Ceres, 4 Vesta
  • Deep Impact: 9P/Tempel 1, Earth/Moon/Jupiter as calibrators
  • Deep Space 1: 9969 Braille, 19P/Borrelly 1, Cometary Dust
  • EPOXI: 103P/Hartley 2, Transiting exoplanet systems; Earth and Mars as exoplanet analogs, Moon; C/Garradd (2009 P1), C/ISON (2012 S1)
  • Galileo: 243 Ida and Dactyl, 951 Gaspra, Small planetary satellites, D/SL9, Interplanetary dust
  • Giotto: 1P/Halley, Interplanetary dust
  • Giotto Extended Mission: 26P/Grigg-Skjellerup
  • Hayabusa: 25143 Itokawa
  • Hayabusa2: (162173) Ryugu
  • LADEE: lunar dust
  • Lucy: Jupiter Trojans
  • Mariner 9: Deimos, Phobos
  • NEAR: 433 Eros, 253 Mathilde, C/Hyakutake (1996 B2)
  • NEOWISE: Survey of small bodies in the IR
  • New Horizons: Pluto, Charon, Kuiper Belt Objects, Earth, Jupiter, Jovian satellites and rings, Interplanetary dust, Solar wind
  • NH Kuiper Belt Extended Mission (KEM): Kuiper Belt Objects, Centaurs, interplanetary medium
  • OSIRIS-REx: Bennu
  • Phobos 2
  • Phobos Sample Return: Phobos, Mars
  • Rosetta: 2867 Steins, 21 Lutetia, 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko, 9P/Tempel 1, C/2002 T7 (LINEAR), Jupiter, Earth/Mars as calibrators; 4 VESTA, 354P/LINEAR 58 (2010 A2), C/MACHHOLZ (2004 Q2), VENUS, MOON, SATURN, M1 PHOBOS, M2 DEIMOS, Pluto
              67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko Observations
  • Sakigake: 1P/Halley, Interplanetary dust
  • Stardust: 5535 Annefrank, 81P/Wild 2, Cometary dust
  • Stardust-NExT: 9P/Tempel 1
  • Suisei: 1P/Halley, Interplanetary dust
  • Vega 1: 1P/Halley, Interplanetary dust
  • Vega 2: 1P/Halley, Interplanetary dust
  • Viking Orbiters 1 & 2: Phobos, Deimos
  • Voyager 1 & 2: Small planetary satellites

Other Spacecraft Missions

These are missions with primarily non-planetary goals but happened to also collect some small bodies data.

  • HST: 1 Ceres, 4 Vesta
  • IRAS: Interplanetary dust, Asteroids, 9P/Tempel 1
  • IUE: 38 comets including 1P/Halley, 19P/Borrelly 1, 21P/Giacobini-Zinner, 9P/Tempel 1, 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko
  • MSX: Interplanetary dust, Asteroids, Comets
  • SOHO: Sungrazing comets; surveys of various short and long period comets
  • Spitzer: comet spectra
  • Ulysses: Interplanetary dust
  • ICE/ISEE: 21P/Giacobini-Zinner

International Observing Campaigns

  • Comet ISON Observing Campaign (CIOC): C/2012 S1 (ISON)
  • International Halley Watch: 1P/Halley, 27P/Crommelin 1, 21P/Giacobini-Zinner 1
  • SL9 Jupiter Impact: Shoemaker-Levy-9 (D/1993 F2)
